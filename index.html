The Structural Barriers

  1. Accessibility and logistics: Inconsistent collection, especially in high-rise and rural areas, makes recycling inconvenient.
  2. Cultural mindset ("Amah Syndrome"): Reliance on contracted cleaners and low ownership of sorting duties encourages wish-cycling.
  3. Policy without teeth: Weak enforcement, limited incentives and missing EPR reduce compliance and producer accountability.

The Cleanup Paradox

Often well-sorted items from community cleanups are co-mingled later because transport/logistics and contracts are not arranged to keep segregated streams separate.

Summary and Way Forward

Malaysia's recycling challenge is systemic: infrastructure, culture and enforcement must be addressed together. Key actions: reliable local collection, public education targeting the cultural gap, and legislation (EPR, PAYT) with enforcement and incentives.
